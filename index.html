Top 15 Movies and Series of all time
according to Luz

Movies

1. Pulp Fiction

From my favorite director, the storytelling in this film is what makes it unique. Many would agree it's an all-time classic. The cast's performances, soundtrack, and screenplay are why this is my number-one movie â€” a must-watch for me.

2. A Star is Born

Originally produced in 1937, this is the 3rd remake, beautifully adapted to our era by co-star Bradley Cooper in his debut as a director. If you love musicals with natural transitions to songs, where singing fits the moment, you'll not only enjoy the film but also get to hear an award-winning soundtrack co-written by the multitalented Lady Gaga.

3. I Origins

This movie dramatically changed the way I see the world. I totally recommend this romantic sci-fi drama.

4. Star Wars Trilogies

I'm a bigger fan of the first trilogy due to its innovation and ambition, but the whole Star Wars universe is something I think is worth exploring.

5. Interstellar

Nolan's cleverness in creating complex stories is fully displayed in Interstellar, which is my favorite of his movies so far.

6. Jurassic Park

One of the first movies that made me feel like a cinephile. Spielberg's classic not only is well-made but continues to thrill my inner child with his astounding use of special effects to bring back to life the magical dinosaur world.

7. The Harry Potter Saga

Growing up alongside the actors and characters of this saga created a connection like no other. This is a magical way to experience a pretty fantastic world.

8. Loving Vincent

A true work of art, this movie is composed of 6,500 frames, each painted manually using Van Gogh's techniques. Experiencing movie's visual story with illusionist artwork is a delight.

9. Arrival

The development of this movie keeps you on the edge of your seat, and by the end, it left me thinking about life and time in a broader context.

10. Back to the Future Trilogy

More than a trilogy, these three movies are so interconnected that I consider them three parts of the same story. True classics that I recommend binge-watching.

11. 2001: A Space Odyssey

Even before Star Wars, Kubrick delivered one of the top sci-fi movies ever. Many films today draw inspiration from this masterpiece.

12. Mean Girls

A classic for my generation. I watch this movie at least once a year around October 3rd. A true comedy classic with 2000s humor that we don't see much anymore. The recent musical adaptation is also worth checking out if you're a fan.

13. La La Land

The movie that made me a Chazelle fan. The attention to detail, not only in mixing the soundtrack with choreography and scenes but also with the color palette, makes it a perfect tribute to many musical classics.

14. The Fourth Kind

When talking about the horror genre, this based on true events movie, is the only one that truly scared me. Its narrative is original and thrilling.

15. Cloud Atlas

This movie tells an enormous story that really got me thinking about how small actions can affect the chaos of the universe we all live on.

Series

1. Friends

I've watched all the episodes at least five times and I never get tired of it. It's my favorite series due to its relatable moments, where friends are also family. As the seasons go by, you fall in love with the internal jokes and characters more and more.

2. GOT & HOD

These HBO adaptations of George RRM's fantastic world mix magic and political power plays with complex character development and action scenes. Based on my favorite book saga, they really make you fear for the protagonists, unlike much others. I fully endorse to dive in in both shows to get the full story.

3. The Good Place

Mixing philosophy, comedy, and thriller, this series ended up giving me some real-life advice.

4. Black Mirror

When talking about dystopian futures and plot twists, I believe there's nothing like Black Mirror. The movie is also a masterpiece, providing an interactive experience.

5. Dark

A must-watch series if you enjoy intertwined stories and sci-fi mysteries.

6. Breaking Bad & Better Call Saul

Considered by many as one of the best, I personally enjoyed both storylines and highly recommend watching them. They're excellent.

7. Rick and Morty

The best adult cartoon in my opinion. Geek jokes, pop culture references, dark comedy, and surprisingly heartfelt moments make this show very enjoyable.

8. The Office (US)

This sitcom is fresh and different from classic formats. For me, it's also an ode to the diversity of human friendships we've almost certainly experienced.

9. The OA

An artistic series that delicately and naturally develops a journey between spirituality and real life.

10. Steven Universe

Although primarily made for kids, this musical cartoon series touches on real topics and taught me a lot about emotions.

11. How I Met Your Mother

The characters of this sitcom are eccentric in the best way. With good humor and unique storytelling, this quickly became one of my favorites while it was still airing.

12. WandaVision

As a Marvel fan, this series had to be in my top choices. What stood out for me was the way the episodes honored sitcom history, but other movies and series are also recommended to get the full context behind this Marvel miniseries.

13. The Bear

The scenes to showcase usual scenarios, highlight art in food and in the city where the show takes place are so exquisite it slowly wraps you around tastefull drama.

14. American Horror Story

This saga has several seasons with diverse stories using a regular cast of professionals that steal your hearth. Each season engages you with new stories.

15. The Big Bang Theory

The jokes and references to the modern world are just the best if you love science, comics, and geeky stuff.
